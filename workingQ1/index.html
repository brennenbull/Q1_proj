One of the most common groups of meteorites. Accounts for approximately 40% of ordinary chondtites. Thought to have originated from three parent asteroids.

Class: H chondrite

Ordinary Chondrites

The most common type of meteorite. Accounts for approxoimately 46% of the the ordinary chondrites. Thought to have come from the same parent asteroids as the L chondrite. The H comes from their high iron abundance.

Class: Ureilite

Rare type of stony meteor

A rare type of meteorite that has a unique mineralogical composition. Named after the village Novy Urey, a town in Russia where this type fell on September 4th 1886. Some examples contain amino acids, the building blocks of life.

Chemical Composition: Olivine, Pigeonite, Carbon

Class: Carbonaceous Chondrites

Comprises 8 known groups and many ungrouped

This class includes some of the most primitive know meteorites. Carbonaceous chondrites are grouped according to distinctive compositions of their parent body. Also found to contain amino acids.

Chemical Composition: Silicates, Oxides, Sulfides, and Water

Class: Shergottites

A sub class of Martian meteorites

Named after the Shergotty meteorite, which fell at Sherghati Indea in 1865. The 3-million-year-old Mojave crater on mars is thought to be the source of the original ejection of these meteorites.

Chemical Composition: Basalt, Olivine, Other rare-earth elements

Class: Iron

Parent class

These meteorites consist overwhelmingly of iron-nickel alloy and are some of the largest found on earth. Iron types are relatively rare but due to their high metal content they survive atmospheric entry and are found in larger chunks. Iron in these meteorites was commonly the earlies source of iron for human use.
